What added value does your work bring to a client?
Dataveyes’ approach does not consist in setting up yet another data analysis tool for the companies it works with. In reality, Dataveyes strives to help their clients better extract value from their data, preparing them to the new data ecosystem. We design and produce advanced human-data interaction solutions, bridging the widening gap between abstract data and the various needs around data : from better comprehension to better innovation. This long term initiative not only requires the advanced interfaces we design; it needs our ability to involve the client all along a project. This can be done by carefully listening to their issues, understanding their business purpose, analyzing their processes and organization structures, conceiving interfaces that efficiently answer their needs and testing the most relevant information experiences
How are you different from business intelligence tool providers?
We believe that the true potential lies at the fingertips of end users, rather than within data centers. We perceive our mission to be at the very end of the data value chain, in close touch with users : this is where data-driven strategies are implemented, and where organization changes can be witnessed. We always keep the final user in mind. Our ambition is to design bespoke interfaces, which will greatly help users grasp, process and understand the data. We favour short, agile work cycles. They allow the creation of more mature tools on an ongoing basis, rather than a long tunnel of development which can divert us from the intricacies of the data. We spend a great part of our time creating interfaces meeting non-standardized user needs. This is how we guarantee long term relevance. We also develop our own proprietary technological components, to capitalize on our research works. We will allow the whole organization to improve its data literacy. Along our creation and production process, we want to ensure that all stakeholders feel more comfortable understanding the data, not only the people in charge of the tool’s administration.
