Frequently Asked Questions
What is user experience design?
Good Question! We have a description on our blog that explains Drew's definition.
What should I expect when I hire Drew Lepp Designs?

You should expect a wonderful, creative and thoughtful experience that your users love.

Be ready to roll up your sleeves because it could also be a lot of hard work. Sometimes we may make recommendations based on the research, expertise and experience that are unexpected, and sometimes, a little scary. At first, being uncomfortable and pushing your product forward is part of the design process but everything we do is ultimately for the success of your project.

Otherwise, be ready to have tons of fun and learn a more than you ever wanted to know about your end-users.

What is the process like?

Although exact process varies from project to project, they generally fall into 3 phases.

  • UX Strategy (1-2 weeks)

    • During the initial consultation, we meet with your team to clearly articulate a few key pieces of information including objectives, goals, target users and content.

  • Research/Discovery Phase ( 2- 4 weeks)

    • We start by looking at what information is already available to us and then decide which pieces are missing. This phase can include user interviews, usability evaluations, and field research with target users.

  • UX Design ( 3 - 6 weeks)

    • This is where your application starts to come together. We create wireframes (and sometimes prototypes) to test out features, functionality and flow.

  • UI Design (3 to 6 weeks)

    • Now, the application is starting to look like a finished product. Based on the needs of your user, we create a style guide to determine the look and feel of the interface.

Who do you work with?
Size doesn’t matter — from large organizations to startups — we’ve worked with them all. What does matter is an eagerness to start building wonderful, user-centered designs together. We consider each job a partnership and work with teams who:
  • Are ready and eager to work with designers to build the best solutions with our combined expertise.
  • Have a development team already in place or are willing to work with one of our talented recommendations.
  • Feel comfortable with the idea of using research to inform and test designs.
  • Are ready to have fun and work hard to build something even greater than you thought was possible!
How long does it take to complete a project?
There are many variables that effect the overall timeline but in general, estimate around 2 to 4 months for a fully designed product or research project. Availability usually opens up within a month of initial contact.
How will we communicate throughout the project?
Communication depends on the team but usually includes a combination of tools like Slack, Trello, Basecamp and Invision to keep files and comments organized online. Depending on your location, we can also arrange in-person meetings on a regular basis.
