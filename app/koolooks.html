Koolooks Social Mobile Application

The backstory

TThe problem: you are headed out to an event and can’t figure out for the life of you what to wear. “Is this skirt too tight? Is this too informal for a nighttime wedding? Does this count as black-tie? Will anyone notice this mustard stain from earlier in the day? Or, the infamous, “Does this make my butt look too big?” Or now, does this make my butt look big enough?

Many people have encountered this problem at some point or another. Right now, most in this situation would text a photo to a trusted friend with a similar fashion sense and hope they reply in time. But isthere another way to use technology to make this easier, more immediate, more helpful, and maybe even more fun?

Enter Koolooks.

Drew's Role

  • UX Strategy
  • UI/ Interaction Design

    • The Process

    I came into my first meeting with the Koolooks team completely blind. I knew almost nothing about the application and knew nothing about the team other than what I could find via Google. But, when I walked into the room for our first meeting, I knew something magical was about to happen.

    In the simplest terms, the app does this: users post two photos of outfits or items and then, other users vote on which they like best.

    As I listened to the company's pitch, the energy of the team was so infectious, my brain immediately started thinking of all the potential directions, and then the potential challenges an application like this might face. An application like this needs daily active users -- and a very specific kind of active user -- to be successful. So we started brainstorming who that ideal user was and how do we reach them.

    We decided to tailor it to a niche market of shoppers who love fashion, beautiful images and helping others by sharing advice. The immediate priorities were designing a welcoming onboarding experience and demonstrating value immediately, even with a small initial network and how to keep users engaged when not actively shopping.

    The client wanted everything in the application to be original with no stock photos (music to my ears!) so we created illustrations and held photo shoots to keep with the overall theme of simple, stylish, and fun.
